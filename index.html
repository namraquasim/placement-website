Food security in the future is an issue that worries the world's governments and scientists alike. During the twentieth century, the population of the Earth increased fourfold, and estimates indicate that the number will reach ten billion people by 2050. This massive increase will represent a major challenge and escalating pressure on agricultural production capacity. This was and should have prompted the development of innovative technologies in the manufacture of food other than agriculture, including a future technology based on the manufacture of food from the air.

Agriculture occupies large areas of land, consumes huge amounts of water, and food production by agriculture contributes to a high proportion of global greenhouse gas emissions.

Food production through agriculture contributes to a high percentage of global greenhouse gas emissions, and for comparison, this percentage of emissions is greater than what is produced by the transportation sector with all its cars, trucks, planes and trains.

Title

Plants obtain their food through a process called photosynthesis, in which plants convert sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide in the atmosphere into food and release oxygen as a byproduct of this chemical reaction. This process occurs in the "chloroplasts". Plants benefit from the energy of sunlight in splitting water into hydrogen and oxygen, and other chemical reactions occur that produce glucose, which they use as a source of food, and oxygen is released from plants to the atmosphere. This means that plants convert carbon dioxide into food through complex chemical reactions. Photosynthesis is one of the most important chemical reactions on the planet, as it helped in the past for the development of our planet and the emergence of life on it. Plants use carbon dioxide to make their own food, and release oxygen to help other organisms breathe!

Sub-address

This process inspired NASA scientists during the 1960s to explore the idea of ​​feeding astronauts on long space missions such as traveling to Mars. One of the most promising ideas was the manufacture of food using carbon dioxide produced by astronauts, but not by plants, but by small, single-celled microbes capable of harvesting carbon dioxide to produce abundant amounts of nutritious protein in the form of a flavorless powder. In making our familiar foods.

Unlike in the plant world, these microbes do not use light as happens in the process of photosynthesis that plants use to obtain food, that is, because they are able to grow in the dark. These bacteria are called "hydrogenotrophs", and they use hydrogen as a fuel to produce food from carbon dioxide. When astronauts produce carbon dioxide, it is captured by microbes and transformed with other inputs into carbon-rich food. In this way we will have a closed-loop carbon cycle.

Sub-address

After more than half a century of NASA research, several companies in the synthetic biology sector, including Air Protein and Solar Foods, are developing a new generation of sustainable food products, without a carbon footprint. These food products will not be limited to astronauts only, but will extend to all the inhabitants of the Earth, and will be produced in a short period of time, rather than months, and without relying on agricultural land. This means getting food products very quickly. It will also be possible to manufacture food vertically from these microbes, instead of the traditional horizontal method similar to modern vertical farming technology. This means providing more food products than the space itself.

Human food consists of three main types:

  • Proteins
  • carbohydrates
  • Fats

Proteins are made of amino acids, a group of organic compounds that number in the human body twenty amino acids, of which nine are essential that the body obtains from food. Amino acids are mainly composed of:

  1. carbon
  2. hydrogen
  3. Oxygen
  4. Nitrogen

It is noted that nitrogen makes up 78% of the air, and hydrogen is obtained through the electrolysis of water, and it is theoretically possible to withdraw carbon from the air to form these acids, because carbon is the backbone of amino acids, and life on Earth is based on Carbon for its ability to form long carbon chains, and this is what microbes do by synthesizing amino acids from carbon dioxide through a set of complex chemical reactions. In addition to making protein-rich meals, these microbes produce other products such as oils that have many uses.

Currently, several companies are studying these microbes more deeply, and are cultivating them for food. In 2019, researchers at the American company (Air Protein) announced their success in converting carbon dioxide in the air into industrial meat made of protein, which does not require any agricultural land, but is mainly air-dependent.

Meat is made of many types

These researchers used air and renewable energy as inputs in a process similar to fermentation, to produce a protein that contains the nine essential amino acids, is rich in vitamins and minerals, and is free of hormones, antibiotics, pesticides, and herbicides.

Meat has been manufactured in many types, including poultry, cows and seafood, without carbon emissions, unlike raising cows, which contributes to the emission of methane, a greenhouse gas.

New feature

December 14, 2020 by Jacob

The Finnish company Solar Foods has also developed a technology for producing protein from air, where the process begins by splitting water into its components hydrogen and oxygen through electricity. Hydrogen provides energy for bacteria to convert carbon dioxide and nitrogen in the air into organic matter rich in protein more efficiently than plants grow using photosynthesis. This protein is similar to wheat flour and has been called "Solein".

The company is currently collecting data about the food product to present it to the European Union in order to obtain a food license, and it plans to start commercial production next year 2021. The company has made it clear that it is interested in producing eco-friendly foods by using basic materials: electricity and carbon dioxide, and these foods will avoid the negative environmental impact of traditional farming that includes everything from land and water use to emissions from fertilizing crops or raising animals.

As such, microbial-derived proteins will:

  • It provides a possible solution in light of the increasing future global demand for food
  • Food factories are expanding in the future to be more efficient and sustainable
  • It will be able to provide food for the astronauts on their journey to Mars and all the inhabitants of planet Earth in the year 2050 AD

Imagine that microbes will be the factories of the future, and that the food of the future will be made of air! And that the year 2050 AD will be completely different from our world today. It is a world without farming or raising animals for food! It may sound fancy, but it's not impossible!
